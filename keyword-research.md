# Prevention-Focused SEO Keywords — healthcare.gammal.tech
 
**Goal:** Target top-of-funnel, informational/prevention searches (people looking to *stay* healthy) rather than transactional/booking searches (people who already have a problem and want a doctor). This builds organic traffic and trust before the person is ready to convert.
 
**Audience/geo:** Cairo & Egypt-focused, English-language queries (add Arabic-language equivalents as a follow-up phase — see note at bottom).
 
## ⚠️ Note on search volume figures
 
I don't have access to a paid keyword-data tool (Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, Semrush, etc.), so I can't pull exact monthly search volumes. The numbers below are **informed estimates** based on:
- Relative popularity of similar English-language health queries globally and in MENA markets
- Typical search patterns for prevention/wellness vs. transactional health content
- The size of the searchable population (Egypt has ~40M+ internet users, with English health searches concentrated in Cairo/Alexandria/urban, English-literate segments)
Treat these as **directional (Low / Medium / High + rough range)**, not verified figures. Before committing budget or content calendar slots, I'd recommend running the final shortlist through Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, or Semrush (all support Egypt as a geo-filter) to confirm real numbers and see related/"people also search" variants.

## Suggested next steps
 
1. **Verify volumes** for the top 8–10 candidates (marked Medium/High) using Google Keyword Planner or Ahrefs with Egypt as the geo filter, before building a content calendar around them.
2. **De-duplicate overlapping intent** — #2, #9, and #17 all target "boost/strengthen immunity"; pick one as the pillar page and use the others as supporting content or FAQ sections.
3. **Add an Arabic-language phase** — a large share of Cairo health searches happen in Arabic (e.g. "كيف أقوي جهاز المناعة"). This list intentionally stays English-only for now; a parallel Arabic keyword list would roughly double organic reach.
4. **Map each keyword to a soft CTA** (e.g. "book a preventive checkup") rather than a hard sales pitch, to stay consistent with the prevention-first positioning.
